Richard Griffiths - Visual CV

After numerous discussions regarding X years of experience*, I decided to kill two birds with one stone.

I wanted to learn to use D3.js and felt I'd like to illustrate the ground I have covered since I began learning to code.

Of course, what is ommitted are the lengths of time where, frankly, I didn't code at all. Either due to my job at the time or because I did actually give up. There I said it. There were times I quit coding for all kinds of reasons. However, as I hope to illustrate, the graphic below will illustrate what experience I have. Like any CV - it is designed to make it easier for potential employers to decide if I'm the right individual to help their business at this time.

Yes, I disagree completely with the black and white filter many recruiters and hiring managers use in this field. I've sat with coders of a decade or two experience and been as equals. I've also sat next to a 17 year old kid who'd got far fewer years experience and was seriously impressed. What you did with your years of experience is far more important than their length!
